What to consider before renting a dumpster?

Dumpsters are not cheap and you don’t want to get stuck with an exorbitant bill for something that may only be used for a short time. If your project is large, it might make sense to rent more than one dumpster at a time. However, if you’re doing work on your own home or business, then one 20-yard roll-off dumpster can likely meet all of your needs.

Make sure the dumpster you choose will be able to accommodate all of the waste you have. You don’t want to fill it up halfway and have to make two trips.

You’ll also want to consider what type of waste you’ll be putting into the dumpster. Some materials, like appliances and electronics, may not be accepted by your local landfill. Be sure to check with your municipality before renting a dumpster.

Be sure to factor in delivery and pick-up fees when deciding whether or not to rent a dumpster. Many companies will charge extra for these services, so be sure to ask up front.
